## Purpose and Scope

ISO 42001 provides requirements and guidance for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an **Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS)** within organizations.

### Key Objectives:

- Ensure responsible development and use of AI systems
- Manage AI-specific risks and opportunities
- Demonstrate compliance with AI governance requirements
- Build trust in AI systems through systematic management
- Support alignment with regulations (EU AI Act, etc.)

### Applicable To:

- Organizations developing AI systems
- Organizations deploying AI systems
- Organizations using AI systems
- Any organization size (scalable approach)
- All sectors and industries

---

## Structure Overview

ISO 42001 follows the **High-Level Structure (HLS)** common to ISO management system standards, making it compatible with ISO 27001, ISO 9001, etc.

### Main Clauses (4-10)

| Clause | Title | Purpose |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **Clause 4** | Context of the Organization | Understanding internal/external context, stakeholders, scope |
| **Clause 5** | Leadership | Top management commitment, policy, roles and responsibilities |
| **Clause 6** | Planning | Risk and opportunity management, objectives, planning changes |
| **Clause 7** | Support | Resources, competence, awareness, communication, documentation |
| **Clause 8** | Operation | Operational planning, AI system lifecycle management |
| **Clause 9** | Performance Evaluation | Monitoring, measurement, analysis, internal audit, management review |
| **Clause 10** | Improvement | Nonconformity, corrective action, continual improvement |

## Detailed Clause Breakdown

### Clause 4: Context of the Organization

**4.1 Understanding the organization and its context**

- Internal and external issues affecting AIMS
- AI-specific contextual factors
- Stakeholder needs and expectations

**4.2 Understanding the needs and expectations of interested parties**

- Identify stakeholders (customers, regulators, employees, society)
- Determine requirements and expectations
- Document compliance obligations

**4.3 Determining the scope of the AIMS**

- Define boundaries and applicability
- Consider AI systems, processes, locations
- Document scope statement

**4.4 AI management system**

- Establish, implement, maintain, continually improve AIMS
- Determine processes and their interactions

---

### Clause 5: Leadership

**5.1 Leadership and commitment**

- Top management accountability for AIMS effectiveness
- Ensure integration with business processes
- Promote continual improvement culture

**5.2 Policy**

- Establish AI policy appropriate to purpose and context
- Commitment to satisfy requirements
- Framework for AI objectives

**5.3 Organizational roles, responsibilities and authorities**

- Assign and communicate roles
- Ensure AIMS conforms to requirements
- Report on AIMS performance

---

### Clause 6: Planning

**6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities**

- **6.1.1 General** - Determine risks and opportunities
- **6.1.2 AI risk assessment** - Identify, analyze, evaluate AI-specific risks
- **6.1.3 AI opportunity assessment** - Identify and evaluate opportunities for AI value creation

**6.2 AI objectives and planning to achieve them**

- Set measurable AI objectives
- Plan actions, resources, responsibilities, timelines
- Monitor and update objectives

**6.3 Planning of changes**

- Manage changes to AIMS systematically
- Consider purpose, integrity, resources, responsibilities

---

### Clause 7: Support

**7.1 Resources**

- Determine and provide necessary resources
- Infrastructure, technology, people, knowledge

**7.2 Competence**

- Determine required competence for AI roles
- Ensure personnel are competent
- Provide training or take action

**7.3 Awareness**

- Ensure awareness of AI policy, objectives, contributions, implications

**7.4 Communication**

- Determine internal and external communications
- What, when, with whom, how to communicate

**7.5 Documented information**

- Create and update required documentation
- Control documents (creation, review, approval, distribution)

---

### Clause 8: Operation

**8.1 Operational planning and control**

- Plan, implement, control processes to meet requirements
- Implement actions from Clause 6
- Control outsourced processes

**8.2 AI system impact assessment**

- Assess potential impacts of AI systems
- Consider ethical, social, environmental factors
- Document assessment results

**8.3 Data management**

- Manage data throughout AI system lifecycle
- Data quality, provenance, security, privacy
- Data governance processes

**8.4 AI system development and deployment**

- Control AI system lifecycle stages
- Design, development, validation, deployment, monitoring
- Version control and change management

---

### Clause 9: Performance Evaluation

**9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation**

- Determine what to monitor and measure
- Methods for valid results
- Analyze and evaluate results

**9.2 Internal audit**

- Conduct internal audits at planned intervals
- Audit program planning and execution
- Report results to management

**9.3 Management review**

- Review AIMS at planned intervals
- Consider audit results, changes, performance
- Make decisions on improvement opportunities

---

### Clause 10: Improvement

**10.1 Continual improvement**

- Continually improve suitability, adequacy, effectiveness of AIMS

**10.2 Nonconformity and corrective action**

- React to nonconformities
- Evaluate need for action
- Implement corrective actions
- Review effectiveness

## Key Concepts

### AI Management System (AIMS)
A management system specifically designed to govern AI activities, including policies, processes, procedures, and resources.

### AI System
A machine-based system that can, for a given set of human-defined objectives, make predictions, recommendations, or decisions influencing real or virtual environments.

### AI System Lifecycle
Stages from conception through retirement:

1. Planning and design
2. Data collection and processing
3. Model development and training
4. Verification and validation
5. Deployment
6. Operation and monitoring
7. Maintenance and updates
8. Decommissioning

### Impact Assessment
Systematic evaluation of potential effects of AI systems on:

- Individuals (rights, safety, well-being)
- Society (fairness, discrimination, social cohesion)
- Environment (energy consumption, sustainability)
- Organization (reputation, legal liability)

## Certification

ISO 42001 is **certifiable** - organizations can be audited and certified by accredited certification bodies.

**Certification Process:**

1. Gap analysis against standard
2. AIMS implementation
3. Internal audits
4. Management review
5. Stage 1 audit (documentation review)
6. Stage 2 audit (implementation assessment)
7. Certificate issuance (if compliant)
8. Surveillance audits (ongoing)

**Certificate Validity:** Typically 3 years with annual surveillance audits
